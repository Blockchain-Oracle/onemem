# Khoj — Visual Design

Captured 2026-05-23. Source-of-truth screenshots in `./screenshots/`.

## Primary screenshots

- `screenshots/01-landing-full.png` — https://khoj.dev full landing

## Brand colors

- Body bg: `#FFFFFF`
- Body text: `#282828` (warm near-black)
- Subdued paragraph: `#676767` (mid gray)
- Almost no chromatic accents visible (could not detect non-transparent section bg colors at all)

## Typography

- Body: `Source Serif 4` (serif!) — Georgia fallback
- H1: `Roboto` (sans), 58px, weight 400 — text: "khoj" (single word, brand mark)
- H2: `Roboto`, 24px
- Paragraph: `Source Serif 4`, 17px, color `#676767`

## Component patterns observed

- Serif body text — unusual for an AI tool. Reads literary / personal-archive.
- No CTA buttons detected on landing (likely linked text)
- Minimal chrome, no cards, no shadows
- Brand name as the H1 only — no marketing tagline overlay

## Layout signature

- Document-style layout, like a personal homepage
- Single-column, narrow text width
- Generous whitespace

## UX moments worth borrowing

1. **Serif body text (`Source Serif 4`)** — uniquely signals "personal", "library", "memory-of-self" rather than "infrastructure dashboard"
2. **Single-word H1 as brand mark** — minimal, confident
3. **Source Serif + Roboto** — both Google Fonts, free, unusual combo

## What this teaches OneMem

- Serif body could be a wedge for OneMem's *narrative trace summary* surface (the part where we summarize what happened in a session in human language). Saves us from the "all sans, all tech" trap.
- Khoj treats memory as personal/literary, not as infrastructure. If OneMem has a "session story" view, serif works for that single surface.
- Most of the rest of OneMem should stay sans (this is infra), but a serif moment is differentiated.
