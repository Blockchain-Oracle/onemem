## Brand colors

- Body bg: `#FFFFFF`
- Body text: `#000000`
- Dark surface: `#292A2B` (graphite — distinctive near-black)
- Section bg variants: `#F9F9F9`, `#E8E8E8`
- Mid-gray: `#666A6E`
- Subdued paragraph: `#BEC4CC` (cool light gray)
- Dark UI: `#222426`

## Typography

- Family: `GT America Medium` / `GT America Regular` (custom — premium grotesk by Grilli Type)
- H1: 68.8px, weight 500 — text: "The easiest way to never forget anything"
- H2: 46.4px
- Body: 17.6px

## Component patterns observed

- Pill-shaped buttons (`border-radius: 40px`) — fully rounded, most extreme in this corpus
- Monochrome palette, no chromatic accent — only graphite + grays
- Dark blocks use `#292A2B` (slightly warm vs Sui's pure black)
- Soft section bg variants for grouping

## Layout signature

- Hero with H1 on dark `#292A2B` block (inverted)
- Alternating light/dark section rhythm
- Wide spacing, generous padding

## UX moments worth borrowing

1. **Fully rounded buttons (40px radius)** — extreme softness, signals "consumer-friendly developer tool"
2. **Pure monochrome palette** — no accent color at all on the hero; brand is the typography
3. **Graphite `#292A2B` instead of black** — warmer, less aggressive than pure `#000`

## What this teaches OneMem

- A pure monochrome marketing page can work if the typography carries the brand (Pieces does it; we'd need a distinctive font).
- Pill-rounded buttons are the opposite end from mem0 / Sui squares — pick a position on this spectrum deliberately.
- GT America is licensed (~$650 per weight); free substitutes: Geist, Inter, Söhne fallback.
