# OneMem — Launch Video · Audio & Sound-Design Spec

Companion to `Launch Video.html` (1920×1080, 30s, 30fps). The HTML carries the
picture and timing; this spec defines the sound a composer/editor lays under it.
Built to the brief's sonic direction: **low mechanical pulse, a short memory-write
click, a soft encrypted sweep, one clean success tone at recall — no loud trailer
impacts, no voiceover in this first cut.**

## Global targets
- **Loudness:** −14 to −10 dB LUFS integrated (social-ad level, matches the X
  references — substantially hotter than the old −25 dB bed). True peak < −1 dBTP.
- **Bed:** one sustained sub/pad in the OneMem key (suggest **F minor**, ~92 BPM so
  beats fall on clean frames). Filtered, never bright.
- **Pulse:** a quarter-note mechanical tick runs under the whole piece as the
  "infrastructure heartbeat"; it ducks under type hits and drops out at the close.
- **Palette:** dry mechanical clicks, granular/encrypted noise sweeps, one bell-like
  success tone, filtered sub. **No** big risers, no white-noise crash, no famous-track
  styling.
- **Mix moves:** sidechain the bed gently to each kinetic-type hit so words "punch"
  the mix. Hard scene cuts get a 1-frame silence pre-roll so the next hit lands clean.

---

## Beat 1 — Agents Forget · 0.0–4.6s
*Picture: scattered memory fragments + four runtime labels in the corners; "Agents
remember." → "Runtimes forget."; fragments collapse to a point.*

- **0.0** Bed fades up from silence; mechanical pulse enters at low level. Faint
  granular "scatter" texture panned wide = the loose fragments.
- **0.25 / 1.75** Two muted **type thumps** (low, dry) on each headline word-set —
  felt more than heard. On "forget." pitch the thump down a tone and let the granular
  texture briefly destabilize (memory degrading).
- **2.3–4.0** Fragments collapse: a short **reverse-suck / vacuum** swell pulling the
  scatter inward, resolving on…
- **~4.3** a single soft **click + sub drop** at the collapse point. This is the cut
  into Beat 2.

## Beat 2 — One Namespace · 4.6–9.8s
*Picture: the OneMem cube assembles; "One memory layer." + MemoryNamespace subtitle;
read/write/share/revoke chips orbit in.*

- **4.6** Cube assembles → one **mechanical "lock/seat" click** as it lands; bed opens
  up slightly (filter lifts).
- **5.4** Type hit on "One memory layer." (the cleanest thump so far — this is the
  thesis).
- **6.8 / 7.0 / 7.3 / 7.5** Four tiny **key-detents** (short, pitched clicks) as the
  capability chips snap onto the orbit — one per chip, ascending.
- Hold a calm sustained pad through 9.8.

## Beat 3 — Decentralized Memory · 9.8–15.4s
*Picture: the stack builds OneMem → Seal → Walrus·MemWal → Sui; words "shared /
encrypted / portable / owned" land beside each layer.*

- Establish a **soft four-step rhythm** (one accent per layer landing, ~every 0.9s).
- **Seal layer** → a short **encrypted "seal" sweep** (granular noise closing, like a
  zip locking).
- **Walrus·MemWal layer** → a low **"store" thunk** (data committed).
- **Sui layer** → a clean **sub-blue tone** on "owned" (the only overtly tonal accent
  here; keep it subtle, Sui-blue = functional).
- The violet spine drawing down can carry a faint rising filter tail tying the layers.

## Beat 4 — Runtime Proof · 15.4–22.2s
*Picture: four evidence cards (Claude writes / Codex recalls / Agents coordinate /
Traces persist) deal in over a slow push-in.*

- Pulse tightens to **eighth notes** — most kinetic section, "proof in motion."
- **One dry "write" click per card** as it lands (4 clicks, slightly varied).
- **"Codex recalls"** card → the **success tone** (clean bell, single hit) on the
  `1 hit` line — this is the emotional payoff of the whole piece: recall works.
- **"Traces persist"** card → a quiet **confirm tick** on `✓ anchored on Sui`
  (verification = a confidence tap, *not* a fanfare — keep it small per the brief).

## Beat 5 — Runtime Surface · 22.2–27.2s
*Picture: "Use the same memory anywhere agents work."; the supported-runtime chip
grid pops in.*

- Bed widens, add a gentle harmonic lift (still no brightness spike).
- **Headline type hit** on the line.
- Chips pop in as a quick **stacc­ato run of soft detents** (7 chips ≈ a fast,
  satisfying "rack of switches" pattern). Resolve, then breathe.

## Beat 6 — Close · 27.2–30.0s
*Picture: OneMem cube + wordmark; lime underline sweep; tagline + links.*

- **27.3** Cube/wordmark settle → one **final low resolving note** (the bed's tonic),
  let it ring.
- **~28.9** The lime underline sweep gets a soft **filtered "shimmer"** pass (short,
  not sparkly).
- Pulse drops out; end on the sustained tonic + a touch of tail reverb. Cut to silence
  on the last frame (no outro sting).

---

## Notes for the editor
- Everything above is keyed to the on-screen timing in `Launch Video.html`; the video
  root stamps `data-screen-label="t=N.Ns"` each second if you need to scrub to a beat.
- Keep verification sounds **smaller** than recall sounds — the story is memory-first;
  proof is the confidence layer, not the headline.
- For the square (1080×1080) and vertical (1080×1920) cuts, the audio is unchanged;
  only the picture reflows.
- No voiceover in this cut. If a VO is added later, duck the bed −6 dB under it and
  drop the pulse to half level during speech.
